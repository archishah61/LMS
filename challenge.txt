1. Task Diversity:
Variety of Challenges: Daily tasks can include:

Quizzes: Short quizzes to check knowledge retention.

Coding Challenges: For programming-focused courses, set small coding problems.

Assignments: More in-depth tasks that require research or written answers.

Peer Review Tasks: Students can review and grade each other’s work to encourage collaboration.

Discussion Prompts: Users can respond to discussion threads related to the topic, fostering a learning community.

2. Points System:
Varying Points: The points system can work like this:

Each task could have points depending on difficulty (e.g., 10 points for a quiz, 30 points for a tough assignment).

Bonus Points: Award extra points for exceptional performance, like completing tasks early, earning a perfect score on a quiz, or contributing helpful insights in a discussion.

Weekly Point Targets: Add a weekly target or goal (e.g., 200 points per week) to encourage steady progress.

3. Streak System:
Loss of Streak: If users miss a day:

They lose 5 points per missed day (or any amount you think fits the challenge).

Partial Streak: If they miss a few days, offer them the chance to regain their streak by completing extra tasks in the upcoming days.

Streak Bonuses: Offer extra rewards:

Completing 5 days in a row could give them 15 extra points.

4. Time-Based Challenges:
Introduce Time-Limited Flash Challenges:

Random challenges that pop up during the week, like a 2-hour quiz or a 24-hour coding sprint.

Users who complete these challenges can earn additional points or special rewards.

Surprise Bonus Points: Occasionally, reward users with points for completing challenges within a specific time frame, e.g., “Complete the quiz within 24 hours to earn +20 points.”

5. Progress Tracking:
Display a progress bar or percentage to show how far the user is into their challenge.

Provide a daily summary of the tasks completed, the points earned, and upcoming tasks, so users always know what’s next.

Personal Goals: Users can set personal goals, like achieving 500 points this week, and track progress towards that goal.

6. Notifications & Reminders:
Set up reminders to nudge users to complete their daily tasks. You can send:

Daily reminder notifications (e.g., “Your challenge for today is waiting for you!”).

Deadline reminders for time-limited challenges (e.g., “Hurry! You have 2 hours to finish the quiz!”).

Offer weekly summaries of progress to keep users motivated to complete the next challenge.
